Custom vs Stock vs Semi-Custom Cabinets in BC: Which Is Right for Your Kitchen?

RSN Kitchen Cabinets Ltd. Updated April 2026 British Columbia

One of the first decisions in any kitchen project is which tier of cabinetry to buy. Stock, semi-custom, and fully custom cabinets each have a place — but the differences in quality, fit, and longevity are substantial. Here's an honest breakdown of all three so you can make an informed decision for your BC home.

The Three Tiers at a Glance

Stock Cabinets

Stock cabinets are pre-built in fixed sizes — typically in 3-inch increments — and stored in warehouses for immediate delivery. You'll find them at Home Depot, IKEA, and similar retailers across BC.

The appeal: Speed and price. If you need cabinets quickly or are working with a very tight budget, stock is the fastest option. IKEA SEKTION cabinets in particular have developed a loyal following for their plywood carcass construction, good hardware, and modular design.

The limitations: Because stock cabinets come in fixed sizes, they almost never fit a kitchen perfectly. Filler strips are used to cover gaps between cabinets and walls — this is acceptable for a budget renovation but looks compromised in a well-finished home. The finish options are limited, and the box construction (especially in non-IKEA options) often uses particleboard, which degrades faster and doesn't hold screws as well over time.

Best for: Rental properties, short-term renovations, and projects with very tight budgets.

Semi-Custom Cabinets

Semi-custom cabinets are factory-made but offer more size and finish options than stock. Many BC kitchen dealers carry semi-custom lines — brands like Aristokraft, Kemper, or Decora — that allow you to specify widths in 1-inch increments and choose from a wider range of door styles and finishes.

The appeal: More flexibility than stock at a middle price point. Semi-custom lines can produce a nicer-looking kitchen than stock cabinets, especially with upgraded hardware and a quality installation.

The limitations: You're still working within the manufacturer's catalogue. Unusual ceiling heights, angled walls, and non-standard features are difficult or impossible to accommodate. Carcass quality varies significantly by brand — some use plywood boxes, many use particleboard. Lead times of 4–8 weeks mean you need to plan ahead.

Best for: Standard kitchen layouts, mid-range renovations, and homeowners who want more choice than stock but aren't ready to invest in full custom.

Full Custom Cabinets

Full custom cabinets are built by a local millwork shop to exact specifications — every dimension, material, finish, and hardware choice is made specifically for your project. Nothing comes from a catalogue.

The appeal: Perfect fit, complete design freedom, and premium construction. In BC homes — where older houses have out-of-square walls, unusual ceiling heights, and non-standard layouts — custom is often the only way to achieve a truly finished result. Custom also allows you to specify premium materials throughout: plywood carcasses, dovetail drawer boxes, full-extension soft-close slides, and whatever door species and finish you choose.

The limitations: Higher cost and longer lead time. Full custom is 6–12 weeks from design approval to installation. It also requires more upfront decision-making — you need to choose all finishes, hardware, and sizes before fabrication begins.

Best for: Quality-focused renovations, non-standard kitchen layouts, high-end new construction, and any project where longevity and design integrity matter.

What About Semi-Custom in BC?

In Metro Vancouver, many homeowners are told they're getting "semi-custom" when they work with a kitchen dealer that sells brand-name cabinetry. In practice, this is factory cabinetry with more options than stock — it's not the same as a locally built cabinet. When RSN builds "custom," every cabinet is built in our Surrey shop by our own crew. There's a meaningful quality and fit difference between this and factory-built cabinetry, even factory cabinetry described as "semi-custom."

A note on BC homes Many Metro Vancouver homes were built before standard cabinet sizing was common. Ceiling heights, wall angles, and kitchen configurations in older Surrey, Vancouver, and Burnaby homes often require custom solutions that stock and semi-custom lines simply can't accommodate well.
