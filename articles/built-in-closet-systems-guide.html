Custom Built-In Closet Systems in Vancouver & BC: A Complete Guide

RSN Kitchen Cabinets Ltd. Updated April 2026 Metro Vancouver & BC

Custom built-in closets are one of the most impactful storage upgrades a homeowner can make. Unlike off-the-shelf systems from big-box stores, a custom build uses every millimetre of your available space, matches the finish of your home's interior, and is built to last decades rather than years. This guide covers what's possible, what it costs, and how to get the most out of a custom closet project in BC.

Walk-In Closets vs Reach-In Closets

The type of closet system you need depends on what you're working with:

Walk-In Wardrobes

Walk-in closets — typically anything over 5–6 feet deep — offer the most flexibility. You can configure full hanging sections on one or more walls, integrate drawer stacks and shelving, add a central island, include a mirror and seating, and build in lighting. Walk-ins in new construction and high-end BC homes are increasingly designed as part of the master suite, with materials and finishes that match the bathroom and bedroom.

RSN builds walk-in wardrobes in everything from painted lacquer to stained white oak, with hardware that matches the door hardware throughout the home. These aren't utility closets — they're finished rooms.

Reach-In Closets

Reach-in closets — the standard bedroom closet with sliding or bifold doors — are limited in depth but can be dramatically improved with a well-designed built-in system. A single 6-foot-wide reach-in with double hanging sections, a drawer stack, and shelving above holds significantly more than the same closet with a single rod and shelf. Custom built-ins make the most of the full height of the closet, including ceiling-height storage above.

Linen and Pantry Closets

Built-in systems also transform linen closets, hallway storage, laundry rooms, and pantry spaces. Adjustable shelving, pull-out drawers, and dedicated compartments for specific items turn a cluttered closet into organized storage. RSN builds these as part of whole-home millwork packages — coordinating styles across all rooms so the cabinetry looks designed, not collected.

Key Design Elements

Hanging Sections

  • Single hang (full-length garments)
  • Double hang (tops, jackets, folded pants)
  • Adjustable rod heights
  • Cedar-lined sections available

Storage Components

  • Drawer stacks (multiple sizes)
  • Shoe shelving (angled or flat)
  • Tie and belt pull-outs
  • Pull-out valet rods

Shelving Options

  • Fixed shelves (strongest)
  • Adjustable shelves
  • Open shelving for folded items
  • Glass-front display sections

Premium Features

  • Central island with drawers
  • Built-in mirror panels
  • LED lighting channels
  • Integrated seating / bench

Materials and Finishes for Custom Closets

Closet systems are built from the same materials as kitchen cabinets. The most common options:

  • White or off-white painted MDF/plywood — Classic, clean look. Works in almost any interior. The most popular choice in Metro Vancouver.
  • Stained wood (maple, white oak, walnut) — Warm, natural feel. Increasingly popular in master bedrooms and high-end homes. Coordinates with bedroom furniture.
  • Two-tone — Darker carcass with lighter doors, or contrasting shelving and hanging sections. Adds visual interest in larger walk-ins.
  • Lacquer finishes — High-gloss or satin lacquer for a sleek, contemporary appearance. Easier to wipe clean than paint.

What Does a Custom Closet Cost in BC?

Custom built-in closet pricing varies significantly by size and scope. Rough ranges for BC:

  • Reach-in bedroom closet (6–8 ft wide): $2,500–$6,000
  • Small walk-in wardrobe (50–80 sq ft): $8,000–$18,000
  • Large walk-in wardrobe with island (100+ sq ft): $20,000–$45,000+
  • Whole-home closet package (multiple rooms): Price varies by scope

These prices include design, fabrication, and installation. Unlike big-box modular systems, a custom build fits perfectly to your space and includes the same quality materials and hardware as the kitchen cabinetry throughout the home.
