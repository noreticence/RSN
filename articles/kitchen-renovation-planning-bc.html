How to Plan a Kitchen Renovation in BC: A Complete Checklist

RSN Kitchen Cabinets Ltd. Updated April 2026 British Columbia

A kitchen renovation is the most disruptive and highest-stakes home improvement project most BC homeowners will undertake. Done well, it adds significant value to your home and improves daily life for years. Done poorly, it overruns budget, drags timelines, and creates headaches that outlast the renovation itself. This checklist will help you plan it right from the start.

Phase 1: Define the Scope (Weeks 1–4)

Before you contact any contractor, get clear on what you're actually changing. Kitchen renovations fall into three broad categories:

  • Cosmetic refresh: New cabinet doors, paint, hardware, countertops — no structural changes. Lower cost, faster timeline.
  • Full cabinet replacement: Remove and replace all cabinetry, countertops, possibly plumbing and electrical. Most common full renovation.
  • Full reconfiguration: Moving walls, relocating sink or appliances, changing the kitchen layout. Requires permits and coordination of multiple trades.

Scope Questions to Resolve Early

  • Are we keeping the kitchen in its current layout or reconfiguring?
  • Do we want to add or remove an island?
  • Are we replacing appliances at the same time?
  • Are we changing the countertop material?
  • Do we want to update the lighting?
  • Is the flooring changing?
  • Are we painting the kitchen or changing the wall finish?

Phase 2: Set a Realistic Budget (Weeks 2–6)

In Metro Vancouver, a mid-range full kitchen renovation (custom cabinets, new countertops, updated fixtures, no layout changes) typically costs $40,000–$90,000. High-end custom renovations with layout changes, premium appliances, and luxury finishes run $100,000–$200,000+.

Allocate your budget across categories before you start getting quotes. A common rough split for a full kitchen renovation:

  • Cabinets and installation: 35–45%
  • Countertops: 10–15%
  • Appliances: 15–25%
  • Labour (plumbing, electrical, tile, drywall): 15–20%
  • Contingency: 10–15% minimum

Do not skip the contingency. In BC construction, something unexpected always comes up — whether it's asbestos in popcorn ceilings in a pre-1990 home, old plumbing that needs updating, or structural surprises behind walls.

Phase 3: Design and Select Materials (Weeks 4–12)

This phase is where most of the decisions are made and where delays happen. The earlier you get a cabinet shop involved, the better — cabinet lead times in BC typically run 6–12 weeks for custom work, and that clock starts only after designs are finalized and a deposit is placed.

Key Design Decisions

  • Cabinet door style (shaker, flat-panel, raised panel, slab)
  • Wood species or material (maple, white oak, walnut, painted MDF)
  • Finish type and colour (stained, painted, natural, high-gloss)
  • Hardware (knobs, pulls, hinges — soft-close is standard in custom)
  • Countertop material (quartz, granite, butcher block, porcelain)
  • Upper cabinet height (to ceiling or with gap)
  • Lighting plan (under-cabinet, in-cabinet, pendants)
  • Storage accessories (pull-outs, lazy Susans, drawer organizers)
Cabinet Lead Time Tip Custom cabinets in BC take 6–12 weeks to manufacture after designs are finalized. This is the most common cause of kitchen renovation delays. Get your cabinet design locked in before you schedule demolition.

Phase 4: Hire Contractors (Weeks 6–14)

For a full kitchen renovation, you may need to coordinate several trades:

  • Cabinet maker / millwork contractor — designs, manufactures, and installs cabinets
  • General contractor — may coordinate all trades if you're doing a full gut renovation
  • Countertop fabricator — templates after cabinets are installed, then fabricates and installs
  • Electrician — for new circuits, lighting, hood fan wiring
  • Plumber — for sink relocation, dishwasher connection, filtration
  • Tiler — for backsplash after cabinets are in place

The Kitchen Renovation Timeline

  • Month 1–2Define scope, set budget, develop design, select finishes
  • Month 2–3Get quotes, hire contractors, place cabinet order (deposit), pull permits if needed
  • Month 3–5Cabinets in fabrication. Coordinate other trades. Order appliances (long lead items).
  • Month 5–6Demolition and rough-in work (plumbing, electrical, structural if needed)
  • Month 6Cabinet installation (2–5 days for most kitchens)
  • Month 6Countertop template and fabrication (1–2 weeks after cabinets are set)
  • Month 6–7Countertop install, backsplash, plumbing connections, appliance install
  • Month 7Punch list, touch-ups, final inspection

This is an optimistic timeline for a renovation with no layout changes. Add 4–8 weeks if permits are required, walls are moving, or if you're coordinating with a condo strata.

Permits in BC

Most cosmetic kitchen renovations (replacing cabinets and countertops without moving plumbing or walls) do not require a building permit in BC. However, if you're moving the sink location, adding a gas appliance, or moving walls, permits are required. Check with your local municipality — requirements vary between Surrey, Vancouver, Kelowna, and Victoria.

Questions to Ask Your Cabinet Contractor

  • Do you build and install in-house, or do you outsource fabrication?
  • What is your current lead time from design approval to installation?
  • What material do you use for carcasses — plywood or particleboard?
  • Do you include soft-close hinges and drawer slides as standard?
  • How do you handle touch-ups or repairs after installation?
  • Do you work with general contractors and designers?
