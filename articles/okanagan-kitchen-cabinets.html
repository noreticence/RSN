How Okanagan Projects Work

Distance from our Surrey shop doesn't complicate the project as much as you might expect. Here's how the process typically works for an Okanagan project:

  1. Initial contact: Email rsncabinets@gmail.com with your project details, drawings, or photos. RSN responds the same day with a scope review and preliminary questions.
  2. Measurements: For projects where site measure is needed, RSN can arrange a site visit to the Okanagan, or work from precise measurements and drawings supplied by the homeowner, designer, or contractor.
  3. Quote and design approval: RSN provides a detailed quote and, where applicable, a design drawing for review. Deposit places the project in the fabrication schedule.
  4. Fabrication: All millwork built in-house in Surrey. Lead time for custom work is typically 6–10 weeks.
  5. Delivery and installation: RSN delivers and installs with our own crew. Travel and accommodation costs for Okanagan installations are factored into the project quote upfront — no surprises.

Design Trends in Okanagan Custom Homes

Okanagan custom home design has evolved significantly in the past decade. The region's wine country aesthetic — warm natural materials, views of the lake and valley, indoor-outdoor connections — translates into particular choices in kitchen and millwork design:

  • Natural wood finishes: White oak, walnut, and alder with natural or lightly stained finishes are popular in Okanagan custom homes, complementing the landscape setting.
  • Warm neutrals: Where painted finishes are chosen, warm whites, greige, and earth tones are favoured over the stark whites popular in urban settings.
  • Open shelving: Kitchen open shelving sections — integrated with closed cabinetry — create a lighter, less box-like feel that suits views-oriented kitchens.
  • Large-scale kitchens: Okanagan custom homes often have more generous kitchen footprints than comparable Metro Vancouver homes, with large islands and extended cabinet runs.
