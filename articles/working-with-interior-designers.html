How Cabinetry Contractors Work with Interior Designers in BC

RSN Kitchen Cabinets Ltd. Updated April 2026 British Columbia

Interior designers working on residential and commercial projects in BC regularly engage millwork and cabinet contractors to execute their vision. The quality of that collaboration — how well the contractor interprets the designer's specs and delivers on them — determines whether the finished project matches what was drawn. This guide explains how RSN works with interior designers and what we need from your team to build to spec, on time.

How Designers and Cabinet Contractors Typically Engage

The designer-contractor relationship in millwork typically starts early in a project — ideally during the design development phase, not after construction has begun. Engaging a millwork contractor early has several advantages:

  • Early pricing allows budget validation before the design is too developed to change
  • Contractor input on constructibility can prevent design decisions that are expensive or impossible to execute
  • Lead times (6–12 weeks for custom) can be factored into the overall construction schedule
  • Material and finish samples can be reviewed and confirmed before fabrication

RSN regularly works with interior designers in Metro Vancouver, Victoria, and the Okanagan — on both residential and commercial projects. The first step is always an email with project details to rsncabinets@gmail.com.

The Design-to-Build Workflow

  1. Initial Brief and Drawings

    Designer submits floor plans, elevations, and any detail drawings. RSN reviews for scope, constructibility, and preliminary pricing. This can start from early schematic drawings — you don't need construction documents to get an initial estimate.

  2. Preliminary Estimate

    RSN provides a preliminary cost range based on the scope. This is used for budget validation. Pricing is refined as specs are finalized.

  3. Material and Finish Selection

    Designer selects wood species, door profile, finish colour, sheen level, and hardware. RSN can provide sample doors and finish swatches for review and client presentation. Selections are confirmed in writing before fabrication begins.

  4. Formal Quote and Contract

    Once specs are finalized, RSN produces a detailed quote. Deposit places the project into the fabrication schedule.

  5. Shop Drawings (if required)

    For commercial projects and complex residential work, RSN produces shop drawings showing exact dimensions, construction details, and elevations. Designer reviews and approves before fabrication.

  6. Fabrication

    All millwork built in-house in Surrey. RSN communicates lead time clearly and flags any schedule changes promptly.

  7. Installation

    RSN installs with our own crew. Site readiness (subfloor level, walls complete, electrical and plumbing rough-ins done) is confirmed before scheduling the install date.

  8. Completion and Deficiency Walk

    Designer is invited to walk the project with RSN after installation to review and log any deficiencies. Touch-ups and adjustments are handled promptly.

What RSN Needs From Your Design Team

Minimum Required for a Quote

  • Floor plan with room dimensions and cabinet location indicated
  • Elevation drawings (preferred) or clear description of each cabinet run
  • Specified door style (shaker, flat panel, raised panel, etc.)
  • Material preference (wood species, painted MDF, etc.)
  • Finish direction (painted, stained, natural) and approximate colour
  • Any special features (pull-outs, glass fronts, integrated lighting, etc.)

For Formal Contract and Shop Drawing Phase

  • Final measured floor plan with confirmed dimensions
  • Confirmed door profile with full spec
  • Exact finish specification — paint code (Benjamin Moore, Sherwin-Williams, etc.) or stain sample
  • Hardware specification — brand, model, finish
  • Confirmed appliance specs (panel-ready appliance dimensions for integrated panel design)
  • Any specific construction details or section views

Millwork Spec Sheets

Many designers work from a millwork specification sheet — a standardized document that captures all cabinet specifications for a project. RSN can work from your firm's template or accept specifications in any clear written format. The key information needed is: cabinet dimensions, door profile, material, finish, hardware, and any interior accessories or special features.

If you're a designer working on your first project with RSN and want to discuss how to format your specs, email rsncabinets@gmail.com — we're happy to review your spec sheet format and confirm what we need to proceed.

Coordinating with General Contractors and Architects

On larger residential projects and all commercial work, there is typically a general contractor coordinating the trades. RSN communicates directly with the GC on scheduling, site readiness, and installation coordination. The interior designer remains the authority on all specification and design decisions, while the GC manages the overall construction schedule.

RSN also reads architectural drawings and can cross-reference structural, mechanical, and architectural information to ensure the millwork is compatible with the overall construction. If you're working from a full drawing set, email it — more information is always better than less at the quoting stage.
