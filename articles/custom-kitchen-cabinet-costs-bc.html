Custom Kitchen Cabinet Costs in BC: A Complete 2026 Pricing Guide

RSN Kitchen Cabinets Ltd. Updated April 2026 Greater Vancouver & BC

If you're planning a kitchen renovation in British Columbia, cabinet costs are almost always the largest line item. Custom kitchen cabinets in BC typically run between $15,000 and $60,000 or more for a full kitchen — but that range is wide for good reason. This guide breaks down exactly what drives the price so you can budget accurately and avoid surprises.

What Does "Custom" Actually Mean?

When contractors use the word "custom," it doesn't always mean the same thing. There are three distinct tiers:

  • Stock cabinets — pre-manufactured in fixed sizes, limited finishes, available at big-box stores. Cheapest option but the least flexible.
  • Semi-custom cabinets — factory-built with some size and finish options. More flexibility than stock, less than full custom.
  • Full custom cabinets — built to exact dimensions and specifications by a local millwork shop. Every dimension, material, door profile, and finish is chosen by you.

What Drives the Price of Custom Cabinets

1. Materials

The box (carcass) material and the door/drawer face material are the biggest cost factors after labour. Solid wood doors in walnut or white oak cost significantly more than maple or alder. Plywood carcasses are more durable than MDF and typically cost more. Premium hardware — hinges, drawer boxes, soft-close mechanisms — adds cost but also adds to the longevity and feel of the kitchen.

2. Kitchen Size and Linear Footage

More cabinets means more material and more installation time. Kitchens are priced largely by linear footage — the total run of upper and lower cabinets. An L-shaped kitchen with an island costs more than a galley kitchen of the same footprint because of the added complexity and island fabrication.

3. Door Style and Finish

A flat-panel slab door in a painted finish is simpler to produce than a five-piece shaker or raised-panel door with a stained finish that requires grain matching. High-gloss lacquer finishes require more prep and finishing coats than a matte paint. The finish adds 15–30% to door cost depending on what's specified.

4. Special Features and Accessories

Pull-out shelving, drawer inserts, built-in organizers, glass-front doors, under-cabinet lighting channels, fluted panels, and decorative columns all add to the scope. Each of these has real fabrication and material cost. When pricing your kitchen, be clear about which of these you want so the quote reflects the actual scope.

5. Labour and Installation

In Metro Vancouver, skilled millwork labour is not cheap. Proper installation includes levelling, scribing to walls and ceilings, fitting around windows and pipes, and making good after the countertop template. Rushing this step results in gaps, unlevel surfaces, and doors that won't stay aligned. Good installation takes time — typically 2–5 days for a full kitchen.

6. Location Within BC

Vancouver and the Lower Mainland have the highest labour costs. Kelowna and the Okanagan are somewhat lower, and Victoria is similar to Metro Vancouver. Projects outside Metro Vancouver may also include travel or delivery costs, which RSN factors into project pricing transparently upfront.

Hidden Costs to Budget For

Cabinet pricing typically does not include:

  • Countertops (quartz, granite, butcher block) — typically $3,000–$12,000 for a mid-size kitchen
  • Appliances — typically $5,000–$25,000+ for a full kitchen suite
  • Plumbing rough-in or fixture relocation
  • Electrical work for under-cabinet lighting
  • Drywall patching and painting after installation
  • Flooring changes

Your renovation budget should account for all trades, not just cabinets. A good rule of thumb is to add 15–20% to your cabinet quote to cover incidental work that arises during installation.

Is Custom Worth the Premium Over Semi-Custom?

For most BC homeowners doing a real renovation — not just a cosmetic refresh — custom cabinets are worth it for several reasons:

  • Fit: Older BC homes in particular rarely have square walls or standard ceiling heights. Custom cabinets are built to fit your space, not worked around it.
  • Quality: Custom shops control material selection. You can specify solid plywood carcasses, dovetail drawer boxes, and full-extension slides — components that are often compromised in semi-custom lines.
  • Longevity: A well-built set of custom kitchen cabinets should last 25–40 years. At that lifespan, the premium over stock cabinets is small on an annualized basis.
  • Design freedom: Custom allows you to fully realize the kitchen you want, not the closest catalogue approximation.
