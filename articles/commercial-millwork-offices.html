What Makes Commercial Millwork Different from Residential

Durability Requirements

Commercial spaces see far more use than a residential kitchen. A dental clinic's treatment room cabinets may be opened and closed hundreds of times per day. A reception desk sees constant physical contact. Commercial millwork is specified with this in mind — heavier-duty hardware, more durable surface materials (often thermally fused laminates or high-pressure laminates in work areas), and robust drawer and door construction that holds up under sustained use.

RSN sources commercial-grade hardware for commercial projects: heavy-duty hinges rated for high cycle counts, full-extension drawer slides with higher weight capacities, and surface materials specified for cleanability and impact resistance.

Coordination with Other Trades

Commercial fit-outs require tight coordination between millwork, electrical, plumbing, flooring, and drywall trades. A dental office needs integrated wire management for equipment, built-in lighting channels, and precise cutouts for sinks and equipment. RSN works directly with general contractors and project managers to ensure the millwork scope is coordinated and on schedule.

Submitting shop drawings, working from architect specifications, and providing material samples for approval are all part of the commercial millwork process. RSN has experience navigating this on both small and large commercial projects.

Finish Standards

Commercial clients often specify finishes to match a brand standard or interior design spec. RSN can match specific paint colours (including proprietary brand colours), produce samples for approval, and maintain consistency across multiple treatment rooms or suites. Matching existing millwork in a phased renovation is also something RSN handles regularly.

Dental Office Cabinetry in BC: Specific Considerations

Dental offices have particularly exacting millwork requirements:

  • Cleanability: All surfaces in treatment areas need to be smooth, non-porous, and easy to disinfect. Certain substrate materials and finishes are preferred over others.
  • Equipment integration: Dental chairs and units require specific clearances, and cabinetry must be positioned and dimensioned to work with the specific equipment being installed.
  • Wet areas: Operatory sinks and sterilization areas require moisture-resistant materials and proper substrate protection.
  • Storage organization: Dental supplies, instruments, and disposables need organized, accessible storage. Custom drawer configurations and pull-out organizers are standard.

RSN has completed multiple dental office fit-outs in Metro Vancouver. We understand the specific requirements and can work from architectural drawings or develop specifications in collaboration with the dental office designer or contractor.
